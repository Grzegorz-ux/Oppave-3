About Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called "The Red Planet" because of the iron oxide dust that covers its surface. Scientists believe Mars once had water and may have supported life in the past.

Today many space missions study Mars to learn more about its climate, geology, and possibility of future human exploration.

Facts about Mars

  • Distance from the Sun: 227 million km
  • Length of a day: 24 hours 37 minutes
  • Gravity: 3.7 m/s²
  • Moons: Phobos and Deimos
  • Average temperature: −63°C
